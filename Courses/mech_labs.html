Mech Labs

Developing practical engineers for machines, workshops, and production systems. The Mechanical Engineering department helps students understand how machines are designed, built, operated, and maintained. The curriculum balances drawing, workshop practice, thermal science, manufacturing processes, and project work.

  • 3 Years Full-time Diploma Program
  • Workshop-focused practical training
  • Hands-on experience with machines, tools, and fabrication
  • Training in manufacturing, quality control, and industrial practices

What Students Learn

Machine Design

Understanding machine components, mechanisms, and working behavior of machines.

Thermodynamics

Study of heat, energy, engines, and thermal systems used in engineering.

Automobile Systems

Basics of vehicle mechanisms, maintenance, and functioning of automobile parts.

Manufacturing

Workshop practice including production processes, machining, and fabrication techniques.

Facilities

  • Mechanical workshops with tools and machines
  • Drawing and design laboratories
  • Practical training sessions and project work

The department focuses on building strong technical skills so students can work confidently in industries, workshops, and production environments.
